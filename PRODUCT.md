# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: campus-recruitment candidates preparing for coding interviews on a 60–90 day runway. They open the app in focused study blocks—often evenings or between classes—with limited daily minutes and a need to know exactly what to do next.

Secondary audiences the product also serves: social-hire switchers on shorter sprints, foundation builders starting from Easy templates, and self-hosting developers who want local data and customizable problem lists. Screens optimize first for the campus prep daily loop.

Job to be done: leave every session knowing which problems to attempt, why they were chosen, and that feedback will reshape tomorrow’s plan.

## Product Purpose

PeachAlgo Coach (黄桃算法教练) is a local-first open-source practice coach. It is not an OJ and does not replace LeetCode judging. It answers three questions: what to practice, when to practice it, and how to adjust after each attempt.

Core loop: onboarding profile → generated learning path → today’s tasks → attempt feedback → adaptive plan + spaced review. Problem catalog stores metadata and outbound links only—no full problem statements.

Success looks like: onboarding to first “today” task in under three minutes; one feedback submission visibly and explainably changes subsequent plans; data stays on the machine with export; daily volume stays finishable rather than shaming.

## Positioning

A feedback-driven local practice coach—not a problem dump, not a LeetCode clone, not a black-box “AI tutor.” Every screen reinforces that completing feedback is what makes the plan smarter, and that the plan lives on your machine.

## Brand Personality

Calm, restrained, coach-like. Voice is steady and specific: short reasons, clear next actions, no pep-talk inflation and no cold dashboard jargon. Emotionally: confidence without hype, discipline without shame—like a coach who pushes you and still respects your limited time.

Three words: **沉稳 · 克制 · 教练感**.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard templates: hero metrics, identical card grids, KPI theater with no coaching voice.
- Gamification medal spam: badges, leaderboards, confetti that distract from practice itself.
- LeetCode / 力扣 visual clones: this is a coach shell, not another problem browser wearing the OJ’s skin.
- Neon cyber-hacker aesthetics: fluorescent terminal stacks that look sharp and read poorly under long study sessions.

## Design Principles

1. **Coach over catalog** — Lead with today’s plan and why; browsing the bank is secondary.
2. **Finishable by default** — Prefer fewer well-chosen tasks over ambitious lists that breed plan shame.
3. **Feedback is the product** — Every attempt path should make submitting result, felt difficulty, and time feel natural and rewarding.
4. **Explain the recommend** — If a problem appears today, the reason should be visible without hunting.
5. **Local trust** — Privacy, export, and “your machine owns the data” should be felt in settings and empty states, not buried in a footer.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline: WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast and interactive targets; full keyboard reach for primary flows (today, feedback, onboarding); honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for any motion. Long study sessions favor readable body contrast over muted-on-tinted panels. Chinese and English UI copy should remain legible at default zoom.
